# Crawl Presets

Use page caps to keep audits repeatable. This skill is page-capped, not fixed-depth: follow valid internal links until the configured cap is reached or no useful pages remain.

## Presets

| Preset | Page Cap | Best For |
|---|---:|---|
| `single-page` | 1 | Fast read of one landing page |
| `architecture-sample` | 20 | Navigation, footer, homepage, top product/pricing/trust templates |
| `site-architecture` | 50 | Mature sites with multiple products, accounts, help, trust, and resources |
| `deep-architecture` | 100 | Large multi-region, marketplace, ecommerce, finance, SaaS, or documentation-heavy sites |

## Selection Rules

- Use `single-page` only when the user provides one page and asks for a narrow read.
- Use `architecture-sample` for quick client prep or executive talk tracks.
- Use `site-architecture` when making implementation recommendations.
- Use `deep-architecture` when the site has many templates, regions, or critical support/legal/resource subdomains.

## Coverage Priorities

Always prioritize buyer-critical pages before long-tail content:

1. homepage, primary nav, footer, sitemap, robots
2. product/service/category pages
3. pricing, account, plan, fee, package, or checkout pages
4. trust, safety, legal, security, compliance, reviews, case, and about pages
5. support, help center, FAQ, docs, setup, and troubleshooting pages
6. comparison, alternative, persona, industry, use-case, and conversion pages
7. education, blog, academy, glossary, news, and resource pages
